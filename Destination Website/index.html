Destination Overview

Islands are host to diverse plant and animal life. Oceanic islands have the sea as a natural barrier to the introduction of new species, causing the species that do reach the island to evolve in isolation.

Humans have lived on and traveled between islands for thousands of years at a minimum. Some islands became host to humans due to a land bridge or a continental island splitting from the mainland.

Islands became the target of colonization by Europeans, resulting in the majority of islands in the Pacific being put under European control.

Activities

  • Snorkeling or Scuba Diving: Explore underwater ecosystems filled with colorful coral reefs, fish, and other marine life. Many islands have rental facilities and guided tours for both activities.
  • Beachcombing and Shell Collecting: Walk along the shoreline to collect seashells, stones, and driftwood. It's a relaxing way to explore and connect with the natural beauty of the island.
  • Hiking and Nature Walks:Many islands have trails that lead to stunning viewpoints, waterfalls, or secluded beaches. You might also spot local wildlife and unique plants.
  • Kayaking or Paddleboarding: Rent a kayak or paddleboard to explore the island's coastline, coves, and lagoons. It's a great workout and allows for peaceful exploration.

Nearby Attaractions

  1. Hidden Beaches:Many islands have quiet, lesser-known beaches that can be reached by hiking or a short boat ride.
  2. Sea Caves and Tidal Pools: Look for sea caves or tide pools along the coastline, which often reveal beautiful marine lifeand rock formations.
  3. Volcano Hiking:Some islands, especially in places like Hawaii or the Canary Islands, have dormant or active volcanoes with trails leading to craters and lava fields.
  4. Scenic Lookouts:Trails often lead to stunning viewpoints or summits with panoramic island and ocean views, perfect for sunrise or sunset watching.
